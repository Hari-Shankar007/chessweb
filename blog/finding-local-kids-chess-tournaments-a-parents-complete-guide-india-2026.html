2. How Registration Usually Works

Registering for a kids chess tournament is surprisingly simple compared to things like school admissions or competitive exams.

Typical registration steps:

    • Age categories (Under-7, Under-9, etc.)
    • Entry fee and payment details
    • Last date for registration
  • Check eligibility:
    • Most local beginner events do not require a rating.
    • Some FIDE-rated or state events require AICF registration and a FIDE ID.
  • Fill an online form (usually a Google Form) with your child‚Äôs details.
  • Pay the entry fee via UPI, bank transfer, or sometimes cash at venue.
  • Save the confirmation message or screenshot for tournament day.

Documents commonly requested:

  • Recent photograph of your child
  • Birth certificate (for age-group events)
  • School ID
  • AICF/FIDE ID for rated events (if your child has one)

For many school-level events, the school itself manages the entire registration. You may just need to give consent and prepare your child for the day.

4. Rules, Time Controls, and What to Expect

Common rules in kids tournaments:

  • For longer time controls, players may have to write moves on a scoresheet.

  • Rapid (10‚Äì25 minutes each): Most common format for weekend kids tournaments.
  • Classical (45‚Äì90 minutes each): Used in serious state and national championships over multiple days.

For younger age groups (Under-7, Under-9), organizers often keep time controls manageable and pairings gentle, understanding that kids need both time and support to navigate a full game.
