Praggnanandhaa Wins Norway Chess 2026: Simple Kids & Parents Guide From India

📅 06/06/2026 • EDUCHESS Team

Summary: Norway Chess 2026 took place in Oslo, Norway from May 25 – June 5, 2026. Six of the world's greatest chess players battled it out over 10 rounds. After an incredible comeback — going from last place to first place — Praggnanandhaa Rameshbabu 🇮🇳 from India won the championship! He beat the world's No.1 player Magnus Carlsen twice and finished with 4 wins in a row. He became the first Indian ever to win Norway Chess. Chess fans everywhere cheered for the 20-year-old hero from Chennai! ♟️🏆

What Is Norway Chess? 🌍

One of the biggest chess tournaments in the world!

Imagine being invited to the coolest chess party on Earth — with only 6 players, all of whom are chess superheroes. That's Norway Chess! It's held in the beautiful city of Oslo, Norway, and this year was its 14th edition. The winner gets a shiny trophy and a big prize!

The special thing about Norway Chess is its format. When a regular game ends in a draw, the two players go to an extra "Armageddon" game — a super fast, super exciting tiebreak where someone must win!

Quick Facts ⚡

  • Dates: May 25 – June 5, 2026
  • Location: Oslo, Norway 🇳🇴
  • Format: 6 players, round-robin (everyone plays everyone twice)
  • Rounds: 10 rounds
  • Winner: Praggnanandhaa Rameshbabu 🇮🇳
  • Prize for 1st Place: 700,000 Norwegian Kroner (about ₹70 Lakh!)

The 6 Chess Superheroes 🦸

  • 🇮🇳 Praggnanandhaa Rameshbabu – India's rising star, age 20!
  • 🇳🇴 Magnus Carlsen – The world's No. 1 player and a chess legend
  • 🇮🇳 Gukesh Dommaraju – The current World Chess Champion from India!
  • 🇺🇸 Wesley So – The super smooth American grandmaster
  • 🇫🇷 Alireza Firouzja – The creative French champion
  • 🇩🇪 Vincent Keymer – Germany's best chess player

Round by Round — The Story of the Tournament 📖

Rounds 1–2: A Shaky Start for Pragg 😬

Pragg started the tournament a little slowly. He drew his first game and then lost to Firouzja in Round 2. After two rounds, he was near the bottom of the table. Many people wondered — could he come back? But Pragg is a fighter. He kept his cool and got ready for Round 3.

Round 3: PRAGG BEATS CARLSEN! 🤯

This was the moment everyone started paying attention. Pragg, playing with the White pieces, defeated Magnus Carlsen — the greatest chess player in the world — in a wild, exciting game full of twists! It was Pragg's first ever classical win over Magnus. The crowd went crazy. Chess fans around the world couldn't believe it!

Rounds 4–6: A Rocky Middle — Pragg Falls to Last Place! 😰

After his big win, Pragg had a tough few rounds. He lost two classical games in Rounds 5 and 6, and by Round 6 he was sitting at the very bottom of the table. Nobody expected him to come back. Even Pragg said later he had to look deep inside himself and keep fighting. The tournament wasn't over yet — but things looked very, very hard.

Round 7: The Comeback Begins! 🚀

In Round 7, Pragg bounced back with a brilliant win. He played with creativity and confidence, showing everyone he wasn't giving up. This was the beginning of his incredible comeback streak — he was about to do something nobody expected!

Round 8: PRAGG BEATS CARLSEN AGAIN! 😱🔥

Pragg beat Magnus Carlsen for the second time in the same tournament! Playing with the Black pieces this time, Pragg kept Magnus under pressure the whole game. In time trouble, Magnus made a crucial mistake and Pragg finished him off perfectly. This was only the second time ever anyone had beaten Carlsen twice in classical chess at the same tournament!

Round 9: Three in a Row! 🎯

Pragg kept the momentum going with win number three in a row! He was now climbing fast up the standings. Wesley So was still in the lead, but the gap was shrinking. The chess world was on edge. Could Pragg actually win it all?

Round 10: The Final Round — Pragg Wins Norway Chess! 🏆🇮🇳

This was it. The biggest game of Pragg's life. He needed a full win (not a draw) to overtake Wesley So and claim the title. Pragg was playing White against Germany's Vincent Keymer. In a Queen's Gambit Declined, Pragg played calmly at first, then launched a powerful kingside attack with 33.f5 and the beautiful 38.Ne6+, cracking open Keymer's king. Keymer resigned — and Pragg was the Champion of Norway Chess 2026!

The Most Amazing Moments 🌟

Moment 1: Last Place to First Place! ⬆️

After Round 6, Pragg was at the very bottom — last place. Then he won 4 games in a row to finish first. This is one of the greatest comebacks in chess history! Even the strongest players in the world struggle to win one game at this level. Winning four in a row is simply unbelievable.

Moment 2: Beating Carlsen — Twice! 👑

Magnus Carlsen is like the final boss of chess. Very few people can beat him even once. Pragg beat him twice in the same tournament — once with White pieces, once with Black! It was only the third time in his career that Pragg defeated Magnus in classical chess, and he did it twice in one week!

Moment 3: The Winning Move — Ne6+! ♞

In the final round game against Keymer, Pragg played the fantastic move 38.Ne6+! — a knight jump that put the king in check, won material, and ended the game. It was brave, beautiful, and brilliant. This move will be remembered for a long time!

Moment 4: History for India! 🇮🇳

Norway Chess has been played for 14 years. No Indian had ever won it before. In 2026, Pragg changed that forever. He became the first Indian champion in the tournament's history — joining legends like Carlsen, Caruana, and Aronian who had won before. India is truly a chess superpower now!

Who Is Praggnanandhaa? 🌟

India's Chess Hero

Praggnanandhaa Rameshbabu — everyone calls him Pragg — was born in Chennai, India in 2005. At just 10 years old, he became one of the youngest International Masters in chess history! By the time he was 16, he was already beating top grandmasters. He is now 20 years old and one of the top-rated players on the entire planet.

Pragg is known for his fighting spirit. He never gives up, even in very bad positions. Norway Chess 2026 showed that spirit perfectly — from last place to champion, he proved that if you keep believing and keep fighting, anything is possible!

What Can We Learn From Pragg? 📚

  • 🧠 Keep believing in yourself — Even when you're losing, never give up
  • 💪 Hard work pays off — Pragg has trained chess since he was a tiny child
  • 🎯 Stay focused — Pragg stayed calm and played his best chess when it mattered most
  • ❤️ Love what you do — You can see how much Pragg loves chess in every move he plays
  • 🌍 Dream big — A 20-year-old from Chennai became the champion of the world's biggest chess tournament!

So next time you sit down to play chess and things aren't going your way, remember Pragg — and fight on! ♟️🔥
